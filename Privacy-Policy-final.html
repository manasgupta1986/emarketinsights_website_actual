Privacy Policy

Effective date: 10 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how eMarket Insights (“we,” “our,” or “us”) collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects personal information when you visit our website, contact us, participate in a study, request our services, or otherwise interact with us. We are committed to handling data responsibly and in a manner aligned with widely accepted privacy, consent, and transparency expectations for websites, advertising platforms, and analytics providers.

1. Who we are

eMarket Insights is a market research and data services company specializing in global fieldwork and data collection, survey programming, analytics, and dashboarding.

Business address: New Delhi, India
Email: info@emarketinsights.in

2. Information we may collect

  • Contact details such as name, email address, phone number, company name, and job title when you submit an enquiry or communicate with us.
  • Study-related information when you participate in qualitative or quantitative research activities that we manage, subject to the notice and consent terms for that specific project.
  • Technical and usage data such as IP address, approximate location, browser type, device details, language settings, referral URLs, and pages viewed.
  • Cookie and similar technology data where these tools are used for website functionality, analytics, security, or consent management.
  • Communication records, including email correspondence and support-related messages.

3. How we use information

  • To respond to enquiries, proposals, and service requests.
  • To deliver research operations, sample management, survey programming, analytics, reporting, and dashboard services.
  • To maintain, secure, and improve our website and service experience.
  • To understand website performance through aggregated analytics.
  • To comply with legal, contractual, fraud prevention, and information security obligations.
  • To manage consent choices where consent is required by applicable law.

4. Legal bases and consent

Where required by applicable law, we process personal data on one or more lawful bases, including consent, legitimate interests, performance of a contract, and compliance with legal obligations. For research participation, cookies, or other optional processing activities, we aim to provide notice and request consent where appropriate. If consent is the basis of processing, you may withdraw that consent at any time, subject to legal and operational limitations.

5. Cookies, analytics, and advertising technologies

Our website may use cookies or similar technologies to operate the site, remember preferences, measure website performance, and understand aggregated visitor behavior. If analytics or advertising technologies are enabled, we intend to implement them in a way that respects applicable consent requirements, user choices, and platform policies. We recommend using a consent banner and region-specific consent handling before activating non-essential cookies in production environments.

  • Essential cookies: required for core website operation and security.
  • Analytics cookies: used to understand traffic patterns and improve user experience.
  • Advertising or remarketing cookies: only if enabled later and subject to appropriate notices and consent controls.

6. Research participant data

When we conduct studies on behalf of clients, the project-specific screener, invitation, consent language, or study brief may contain additional privacy information. Depending on the project, we may act as a processor/service provider handling data on behalf of a client, or as an independent controller for operational purposes. We encourage clients to ensure that recruitment, consent, incentives, transcription, storage, and reporting requirements are clearly documented for each project.

7. How we share information

  • With trusted service providers who support hosting, analytics, communications, security, survey operations, and data processing under appropriate contractual safeguards.
  • With clients when necessary to perform contracted market research or analytics services, subject to confidentiality and project terms.
  • With regulators, courts, law enforcement, or advisers when disclosure is legally required or reasonably necessary to protect rights, safety, or compliance interests.
  • As part of a corporate transaction such as a merger, acquisition, or reorganization, subject to appropriate protections.

8. International data transfers

Because eMarket Insights supports global fieldwork and data operations, personal data may be processed in multiple jurisdictions. Where required, we seek to implement appropriate transfer safeguards, contractual measures, access controls, and internal data handling standards when information moves across borders.

9. Data retention

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose collected, including service delivery, legitimate business operations, contractual commitments, dispute resolution, record-keeping, and legal compliance. Retention periods may vary depending on whether the data relates to website enquiries, research participation, accounting records, or security logs.

10. Security

We use commercially reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect personal information from unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, or disclosure. No transmission or storage system can be guaranteed to be completely secure, so users should avoid sending sensitive information through unsecured channels unless specifically instructed.

11. Your rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have rights to access, correct, update, delete, restrict, object to, or export certain personal information, as well as the right to withdraw consent and lodge a complaint with a competent supervisory authority. To exercise a request, please contact us at info@emarketinsights.in. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request.

12. Children’s privacy

Our website and services are not directed to children unless a specific research project explicitly permits it with appropriate legal safeguards and parental or guardian consent where required. If you believe a child has provided personal information without proper authorization, please contact us so we can review and take appropriate action.

13. Third-party links

Our website may include links to third-party websites or tools. We are not responsible for the privacy, security, or content practices of those third parties. Users should review the privacy notices of external services independently.

14. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect legal, operational, or technical changes. The updated version will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.

15. Contact us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or our privacy practices, contact us at info@emarketinsights.in or write to us at New Delhi, India.
